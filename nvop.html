National Veterans Outreach

Responsive Website Redesign

Challenge

Disabled Veterans are struggling to obtain resources on finding a new career path, receiving housing assistance and co-existing in civilian life. Take the National Veterans Outreach website content and reuse it to make a responsive design that encouraged veterans to find the services they needed.

Sector : Nonprofit, Veterans’ services

My Role : User Research, UX design, Prototyping & Testing

Duration : 2 Weeks

The Process

We started our design process by gathering data from user interviews and analyzing them to find elements that would need to be user-centered.These features were brought to life in our early sketches, from which we iterated them into responsive mobile and desktop wireframes.

After further iterations, applied styling and a series of user tests, we finished with a high-fidelity prototype that we were confident met the requirements of the veterans’ challenge

User Research

We conducted user interviews with 7 veterans and also received 35 responses for survey questionnaire. Also conducted competitor analysis to find out the services available and how they are supporting user needs.

    Key Findings:
  • A noticeable percentage of veterans know how to access only half of their benefits and some do not know at all.
  • Most of them never used job placement or training services

These findings helped us envision the strategy for website redesign.

Qualitative Interviews

User research was quintessential. We gathered as much as information we could from the end users to understand the challenges they face and how they see the website redesign making a difference in optimizing pain areas.

“People love and respect me for my service to our country, I am disabled, so now what?!”

Angela, age 42

“Civilians don’t understand me and it is keeping me from making connections and getting a job.”

Robert, age 38

“There should be more veterans in civilian jobs. What we bring to the table is disciple and strong work ethics.”

Andrea, age 55

Insights

From the interview, survey,contextual inquiry and analyzing the gathered data, we were able to categorize the insights into these 4 categories, all related to employment support.

Low skilled jobs

Veteran under employment

Military skill translation

Lack of clear pathways

To further strengthen the research, we mapped a User persona to empathize with our users.

How might we make veterans feel confident in finding the services they need?

Ideation Design & Iteration

Competitor analysis conducted provided strategic insights into features, functions, flows and feelings evoked by design solutions of competitors.

MVP defined based on the most beneficial and critical actions users could take, created User Flows for employment by matching the skills with career oppertunities. We started to sketch the ideas with a mobile first approach to verify the concept, iterated to low-fidelity prototype and conducted usability testing.

From the feedback, we improved on designing the much more meaningful user interactions by increasing affordances, content prioritization etc and incorporated them to a mid-fidelity prototype.

Finding a Visual Style

We took the organization’s mission statement and the brand attributes to design a style guide which is simple giving more freedom, affordances and confidence to users.

Usability Testing & Findings

Feedback received from 10 users in Usability Testing were prioritized to refine the high fidelity prototype.

The Solution

Implemented a mobile first approach by incorporating fluid grids, accommodating the breakpoints for desktop and mobile. Minimalist elements and interactions were designed giving priority to the content. Here you can see the website as it is before the redesign

Lessons Learned

We have done 3 to 4 iterations to the prototype based on user feedback. Our testing shows the users enjoyed the redesigned website on both mobile and desktop.

Conclusion

  • Seek feedback early and continually.
  • As a designer, we tend to get creative by attractive, trendy designs. But, We must always remember the ‘why’. The primary goal is to understand the user, their problems and then come up with a design that solves it.
  • Create a strategic plan for MVP to eliminate scope creep and helps deliver a quality product in time.

Next Steps

    Much better user experience can be provided with added features like:
  • User Profiles for login, saving the job searches and preferences.
  • Exclusive mobile app for better notifications and location services.
  • Streamline skill match questionnaire.
